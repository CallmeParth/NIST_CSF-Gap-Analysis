# Enterprise Cybersecurity Gap Analysis & Audit (NIST CSF)

Welcome to **Enterprise Cybersecurity Gap Analysis & Audit (NIST CSF)** Repositry ! 🔐

This project simulates a comprehensive, baseline cybersecurity audit and gap analysis for QuickCart Delivery, a hyper-local grocery delivery startup with 35 employees. 
As a fast-growing business handling sensitive customer data (Personally Identifiable Information - PII) and payment tokens, QuickCart lacks a dedicated cybersecurity framework.

Using the globally recognized NIST Cybersecurity Framework (CSF) v1.1, I evaluated the company's current security architecture across 7 core control criteria, calculated an objective maturity score, and authored a professional executive remediation strategy.

----

## 🛠️ Skills & Frameworks Demonstrated

- **GRC Frameworks**: NIST Cybersecurity Framework (CSF) v1.1 (Identify, Protect, Detect, Respond, Recover)

- **Risk Management**: Compliance auditing, data translation, threat exposure analysis

- **Data Tools**: Microsoft Excel / Google Sheets (Average maturity calculations, conditional formatting risk tracking)

- **Business Communication**: Executive report writing, technical documentation, prioritization mapping (Remediation Roadmap)

## 📊 Core Audit Findings

- **Overall Corporate Maturity Score**: 1.86 out of 5

- **Primary Strength**: RECOVER (Score: 3.0/5) — Automated weekly cloud database backups are successfully operational.

- **Critical Exposure Area**: RESPOND (Score: 1.0/5) — Total absence of a formalized incident response playbook or escalation tree.

### 🚥 High-Priority Security Gaps Identified:
- **Unmanaged Hardware Inventory (Score: 1/5)**: Ad-hoc procurement of endpoints without a centralized tracking asset ledger creates visibility blind spots.

- **Fragmented Identity Management (Score: 2/5)**: Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) is only enforced for administrators, leaving standard corporate email accounts heavily exposed to credential-theft/phishing.

- **No Incident Response Readiness (Score: 1/5)**: Lack of a written incident mitigation playbook increases the risk of prolonged operational downtime during a potential cyber incident.

## 📈 Executive Action Plan & Roadmap

The project includes a multi-phase, low-overhead remediation roadmap designed to systematically mature QuickCart's security posture from 1.86 to a target state of 3.0+:

### Phase 1 (Immediate - Next 30 Days | Zero Budget): 
- **Enforce a global policy for baseline MFA across all corporate email profiles and draft a 1-page emergency incident contact tree**.

### Phase 2 (Short-Term - Next 90 Days | Low Cost): 
- **Establish a master hardware asset inventory sheet and transition standalone antivirus endpoints into a unified cloud monitoring console**.
